## One-line goal

Site the designed negative M0.4 relocated off the terminal anchor: **exhibit, as a computed
statement (not a vibe), the dim-3 phenomenon a single deterministic Phase-7 law provably
cannot capture** — crepant-resolution non-uniqueness (flops) of canonical, non-terminal
cyclic quotients. If it dissolves under precision (as Wall 2 did at the terminal anchor),
record *that* honestly — the negative-of-the-negative is a first-class outcome.

## Where M2 sits (value layer; the M1 contrast; the Phase-8 hinge)

- **Value layer.** M2 is the **value-layer** counterpart of M1's endpoint result. M1/H-M1e:
  the *terminal* anchor has a **unique** resolution (one endpoint — the value layer is
  degenerate; the interesting content there is process-layer, the sibling
  `fdrs-resolution-trajectories` T3). M2 asks whether **canonical, non-terminal** quotients
  make the *value layer itself* non-unique (many endpoints).
- **The through-line.** Loss-of-canonicity in dim 3 is a **canonical-vs-terminal** phenomenon,
  not merely dimensional: terminal → `N=1` (H-M1e); canonical Gorenstein → `N≥1`, and the
  no-go is exactly where `N>1`.
- **The Phase-8 hinge (conjecture, not assumed).** H-M1f found the terminal law needs *no*
  Phase-8 routing (tree-state suffices). M2's crepant flop-class — `N>1` resolutions, flop-
  connected — is the natural candidate for where **Phase-8 multi-timeline routing becomes
  necessary**: the flop-class *is* a multi-timeline, flops *are* the routing. So the no-go for
  a single Phase-7 law is simultaneously the **positive case for Phase-8**. Graded conjecture.

## The object

Gorenstein cyclic 3-fold quotients `¹⁄ᵣ(a₁,a₂,a₃)`, `G=ℤ/r ⊂ SL(3,ℂ)` (i.e. `a₁+a₂+a₃ ≡ 0
mod r`), that are **canonical but not terminal** (Reid–Tai age `≥1` all `k`, `=1` for some
`k` ⇒ a crepant/junior divisor exists). *Grounding pending:* the crepant resolutions of such a
quotient correspond to **unimodular triangulations of the junior simplex** `Δ` (the age-1
lattice points on the plane `Σ=1`), pairwise connected by **flops** = bistellar flips
(Nakamura; Craw–Reid, *How to calculate A-Hilb ℂ³*; Ito–Reid; Bridgeland–King–Reid gives
`G`-Hilb as *one* distinguished crepant resolution). `N :=` #(such triangulations) = #(crepant
resolutions).

## Oracle (exactness ladder; enumeration-cost policy pre-committed)

- **Sage:** build `Δ` (junior points via age=1), count its unimodular triangulations
  (streaming `.triangulations()` + unimodular filter — the memory-safe pattern that H-M1e's
  wall forced). Age / Gorenstein / crepant arithmetic exact.
- **Rust `no_std` mirror:** age, Gorenstein test, junior-point enumeration, and the
  small-`Δ` triangulation count where feasible (integer 3×3 dets).
- **Enumeration-cost policy (as H-M1e):** exact triangulation count up to a stated feasibility
  bound; above it, **"untested by enumeration for this case"** — never silently assumed. Bound
  fixed at registration after a feasibility probe (the junior `Δ` is a 2-simplex with interior
  points; counts grow with #junior points).
- **Independent cross-check:** where available, compare `N` against published crepant-
  resolution counts / `G`-Hilb data (grounding pass) — a second oracle for small `r`.

## Results (executed 2026-07-07) — the no-go HOLDS; OB-1 DISCHARGED

**Verdict table**

| Hyp. | Result |
|---|---|
| **H-M2a** | **PASS** — every nontrivial Gorenstein cyclic 3-fold quotient is canonical-not-terminal with a crepant divisor (grounded: age ∈ {1,2} ⇒ age≥1 canonical, and a junior always exists; Rust-verified `age_num ∈ {r,2r}` + junior-exists on 10 anchors). |

**Grounding (sourced).** Crepant divisor ⇔ age-1 (junior) element (Ito–Reid, arXiv:alg-geom/
9411010, Thm 1.4); Gorenstein ⇔ `Σaᵢ≡0 mod r` ⇔ `G⊂SL(3)` (Watanabe; Ito–Reid); **{crepant
resolutions} ↔ {unimodular triangulations of the junior simplex using all junior points} =
{fine triangulations}** (in dim 3 fine ⇔ unimodular, by Pick — Dais–Haus–Henk §5, Lemma
5.1(ii); Nakamura; Craw–Reid, arXiv:math/9909085); `N>1` proven & exemplified (Craw–Reid §1.5:
`1/6(1,2,3)`, `1/11(1,2,8)` have flops); flop-connectivity (Dais–Haus–Henk; Kawamata/Kollár);
`G`-Hilb = one distinguished crepant resolution (BKR, arXiv:math/9908027); every *projective*
crepant resolution of abelian `C³/G` = a moduli `M_θ`, VGIT/flop-connected to `G`-Hilb
(Craw–Ishii, Duke 124, 2004).

**Oracle & validation (exactness ladder).** `N` = #(fine triangulations of the 2D junior
configuration), computed in Sage (`source/sage/m2_crepant.sage`). **Validation: my oracle
independently reproduces every grounded count — 10/10** (`1/6(1,2,3)=5`, `1/8(1,2,5)=4`,
`1/11(1,2,8)=5`, `1/9(1,2,6)=6`, `1/13(1,3,9)=7`, `1/12(1,4,7)=35`, and the `N=1` cases). Four
independent instruments agree on `N`: this Sage oracle + the grounding's flip-BFS enumerator +
its separating-axis brute force + Craw–Reid's qualitative flop statements. Rust
(`source/rust/mcf-radix` `m2` module) mirrors the integer-exact substrate — junior point sets,
age, Gorenstein — and confirms junior-count parity on the 10 anchors. *(`N` itself is a
triangulation count, not integer arithmetic, so Rust verifies its input, not the count.)*

**Battery.** Gorenstein cyclic `¹⁄ᵣ(1,a,r−1−a)`, `a↔r−1−a` collapsed, `3≤r≤17` complete +
`r=18` partial (`runs/2026-07-07-m2-crepant`):
- **smallest `N>1`: `¹⁄6(1,2,3)`, `N=5`** — the worked headline (7 junior points; 5 flop-
  connected crepant resolutions);
- `N` grows fast: `¹⁄10(1,4,5)=31`, `¹⁄12(1,3,8)=104`, `¹⁄15(1,5,9)=717`, `¹⁄18(1,5,12)=945`;
- `N=1` for the Dais–Henk unique series `¹⁄ᵣ(1,1,r−2)` (sourced) and other degenerate junior
  simplices (the symmetric `¹⁄ᵣ(1,a,a)=¹⁄ᵣ(1,(r−1)/2,(r−1)/2)`, and a few like `¹⁄7(1,2,4)`);
  the precise `N=1` set is *not* claimed (it is the "junior simplex has a unique triangulation"
  locus). Bonus: the `a↔a⁻¹` duality preserves `N` (e.g. `¹⁄11(1,2,8)` and `¹⁄11(1,4,6)` both `5`).
- **Enumeration wall** (same as H-M1e, per the same policy): fine-triangulation enumeration
  exceeds compute at large `r` (killed ~`¹⁄18(1,8,9)`); `r≤17` complete, `r=18` partial, above
  **untested by enumeration** — recorded, not trimmed. The verdict does not depend on it.

**The no-go, stated (H-M2b).** For a canonical Gorenstein cyclic quotient with `N>1` crepant
resolutions (e.g. `¹⁄6(1,2,3)`, `N=5`; up to `945`), a **single-valued deterministic Phase-7
law can output at most one** — it provably cannot represent the `N`-element, flop-connected
crepant-resolution class. This is the dim-3 phenomenon the M0.4 designed negative pointed at,
now **sited with a computed multiplicity and a worked example** — the Catalan-no-go analog for
this family. It lands exactly off the terminal anchor (which had `N=1`), confirming
loss-of-canonicity is a **canonical-vs-terminal** phenomenon.

**The Phase-8 hinge (graded conjecture, `F-M2-phase8-vibe` honored).** The flop-class is a
genuine multi-timeline and the flops are its transitions; Craw–Ishii's VGIT/wall-crossing
description makes "routing between the `N` resolutions" precise. So the no-go for a single
Phase-7 law is the **positive case for Phase-8 multi-timeline routing** — *stated as a
conjecture*, not asserted: whether the corpus's Phase-8 routing semantics are *required* (vs.
a plain unordered set of `N` Phase-7 outputs) needs a routing/coupling criterion, the natural
M3 question — the mirror of H-M1f's discipline.

**OB-1 — DISCHARGED.** The designed no-go relocated off-anchor at M0.4 is sited: crepant
non-uniqueness is real (`N>1`, computed, four-way cross-checked), flop-connected (grounded),
with `¹⁄6(1,2,3)` (`N=5`) the smallest worked case. The family's standing obligation is met.
*(Had `F-M2-unique` fired — `N=1` everywhere — OB-1 would have discharged the other way; it did
not.)*
